Venus

Venus, named after the Roman goddess of love and beauty, is often called the Earth's "sister planet" due to its similar size, composition, and proximity to the Sun. However, that is where the similarities end. Venus has a thick, toxic atmosphere that traps heat and creates a greenhouse effect, making it the hottest planet in our solar system. In fact, the temperature on Venus can reach up to 864 degrees Fahrenheit (462 degrees Celsius), which is hotter than the surface of Mercury, the closest planet to the Sun. Additionally, Venus has the longest day of any planet, lasting around 243 Earth days, while its year is shorter than its day, lasting only around 225 Earth days.
